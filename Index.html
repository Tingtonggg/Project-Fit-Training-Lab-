INTRO · 01

Why Aren't You Seeing Results?

Most people stall because of just 3 things:

  • ✕
    Training hard but not controlling calories
    → wasted effort
  • ✕
    Eating little but not enough protein
    → you're losing muscle
  • ✕
    Everything right but no consistency
    → even the best plan fails

Master These 3 Variables

①

Calories

Determines whether you're building or cutting

②

Protein

Determines how much muscle you preserve

③

Carbohydrates

Determines your training performance

Fats · Meal Frequency · Timing → Advanced optimization, not the deciding factors

FOUNDATION · 02

Energy Balance — The Foundation

BULK
+5~15%

above maintenance calories — maximize lean body mass gain

CUT
−15~25%

below maintenance calories — minimize muscle loss

Monitor & Adjust

Adjust ±100–200 kcal weekly based on body weight and training performance.

Energy Intake vs. Lean Body Mass Change

-30% (Aggressive Cut)+20% (Bulk)

Source: Aragon et al., ISSN Position Stand: Diets & Body Composition (2017).

Optimal range for maximizing MPS · Benefits plateau above 2.2 g/kg

Sources: Morton RW et al., Br J Sports Med (2018); Aragon et al., ISSN (2017).

MACRO 2 · 05

Carbohydrates — Training Fuel

Peri-Workout Carbohydrate Strategy

01
1–2h before training

Complex carbs + protein to top up glycogen

02
0–2h post-workout

Fast carbs + protein to accelerate recovery

03
4–6h post-workout

1.0–1.2 g/kg/h until glycogen is fully replenished

Post-Workout Glycogen Resynthesis Rate

Immediate carb intake vs. 2h delay: glycogen resynthesis rate is ~2× faster (Ivy 1988)

Sources: Ivy et al., J Appl Physiol (1988); Burke et al., J Appl Physiol (2017).

20–30%
of total calories

Optimal range — balances satiety and hormonal function

⚠️
Min 0.5 g/kg/day

Absolute floor — risks increase below this level

Diversify Your Sources

Dietary Fat % vs. Testosterone Levels

Fat intake <20% of total calories → testosterone drops ~10–15% on average

Source: Whittaker J & Wu K, J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol (2021).

PART II

The Truth About Spot Reduction

"There's no such thing as spot reduction" is an oversimplification. The real science is more nuanced.
Some areas are very hard to lose fat from — but not impossible.
The key is LPL activity differences.
PART II · 11

What Is LPL (Lipoprotein Lipase)?

ONE-LINE DEFINITION

LPL = The key enzyme on the surface of fat cells

CORE FUNCTION
→

Breaks down blood triglycerides into fatty acids

→

Allows fat cells to absorb and store these fatty acids

🔑
LPL = The "Gatekeeper" of fat storage

The more active it is, the easier fat accumulates in that area

Visual: LPL Differences Across Body Regions

🏋️

High LPL Activity

Fat Accumulation Zone — fatty acids continuously enter cells, cells expand

✨

Low LPL Activity

Easy-Loss Zone — gate opens less; fat accumulates slowly and is lost faster

Source: Mead JR et al., J Mol Med (2002).

PART II · 12

Why Are the Abs & Thighs Hardest to Lose?

THE MORE PRECISE SCIENTIFIC CONCLUSION

Spot reduction is "very difficult, but not entirely impossible"

Key mechanism: Fat cells in different body regions have varying LPL activity. Higher LPL activity → easier fat accumulation, harder fat loss.

SUMMARY · 13

Summary + Strategies for Stubborn Fat

"You can make a region more prone to fat loss, but you cannot spot reduce"
Why belly fat is triple-protected:
α2 receptors ↑ LPL activity ↑ Insulin inhibition ↑

Attacking Stubborn Fat — Combination Strategy

01
Prioritize Strength Training

Increase local blood flow to mobilize fat

02
Do Cardio After Lifting

Burn the already-mobilized fatty acids

03
Manage Carbs / Insulin

Reduce LPL fat-storage signaling

04
Avoid Overeating

Prevent LPL from aggressively storing fat

STRATEGY PRIORITY HIERARCHY
FOUNDATION — Must Do
①Caloric deficit (−15 to 25%)
②High protein (2.3–3.1 g/kg/day)
③Consistent strength training
OPTIMIZATION — Layer 2
Nutrient timing · Meal distribution (3–5×/day)
Diet breaks every 8–10 weeks (MATADOR)
FINE-TUNING — Local Tactics
Strength → Cardio sequencing · Carb/insulin management · Avoid LPL spikes
⚠ Local tactics only amplify results when the foundation is already solid.
